Providing income as a basic human right

"We look to provide personalised support to rough sleepers in Dublin using long-term universal basic income"

As of January 2019, Ireland has reached the highest levels of recorded homelessness in the last 100 years. With over 10,000 homeless - 3,784 of which are children - we need to see a change in policy both at the level of government and in the structure of aid provided by charities and integrated care givers.

With growing research supporting the application of the economic principles laid by Universal Basic Income to challenging homelessness we want to stem the tide of rough sleepers in Dublin through personalised financial support.
